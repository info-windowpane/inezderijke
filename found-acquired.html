The 'Birdman' is a Taíno sculpture of a Zemí (deity or ancestral spirit), made in Jamaica between c.1029—1156. It is one of very few surviving Taíno objects from this period.
It was taken from a cave in Jamaica by trader/collector David Rebello in 1792 and later acquired by the British Museum.
The Jamaican government has made efforts to repatriate Taíno sculptures but the UK refuses to return them, citing the British Museum Act of 1963. No British Museum director has ever lobbied for changes to this law. https://vimeo.com/1080774194
